Full Stack memory-box

a journalling app

This project is a responsive journalling app that asks users a daily question and presents previous answers back to them in various ways. This was a team project that was the culmination of all of the skills we had learned on the course at Northcoders

Here is a link to the Github repo

Here is a link to our final presentation on YouTube (I was at the end)

As a team, we decided to push ourselves and learn some new tech for our project. With only around 10 days to code the entire app, we had to learn Svelte (with SvelteKit) and Firebase. We also used TypeScript throughout the app

We initially wanted to use Svelte-Native with NativeScript but during the spiking phase of our project, we found that unfortunately we couldn't get it to work on our machines for various reasons

Once we got through some teething issues setting up a connection to our Firestore database, we found the development experience with Svelte to be very enjoyable. I hope that it is the future of web-development (compilers vs virtual DOM) and it gains more support in the future

Working with the team I worked with on this project was a great experience which I will remember fondly

Working as a team of 4, we needed to learn some soft skills to ensure smooth development of out app. Such skill include: Husky & esLint (to help reduce merge conflicts), Kanban boards (we used Trello), Figma (for wire framing), daily stand-ups, pomodoro timers (for productivity) and a mix of solo / pair programming & mob de-bugging
